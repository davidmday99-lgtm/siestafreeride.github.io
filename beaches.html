Siesta Key Beaches — Free Ride & Shuttle Guide

Everything you need to know about Siesta Key's beaches — from the world-famous #1 rated Siesta Beach to the hidden gem at Midnight Pass. Parking tips, water conditions, and beach rules included.

The Beaches

🏖️ Siesta Beach — #1 in the USA

Consistently ranked the best beach in the United States, Siesta Beach is famous for its 99% pure quartz crystal sand — so fine and white it squeaks underfoot, and stays cool even on the hottest days.

  • ✅ Lifeguards on duty: 10am – 5pm daily
  • ✅ Free parking (800+ spaces)
  • ✅ Concession stands, volleyball courts, playground
  • ✅ Picnic pavilions and restrooms
  • ✅ Open 8am to sunset
  • ⚠️ Parking fills by 9am in peak season

🐢 Turtle Beach — Dog Friendly

Located on the south end of the Key, Turtle Beach is the locals' favorite. More secluded, less crowded, and one of the only beaches in Sarasota County where dogs are welcome.

  • ✅ Dogs allowed on leash
  • ✅ Great for shelling and fossil hunting
  • ✅ Boat ramp access
  • ✅ Campground nearby
  • ✅ Picnic tables and grills
  • ⚠️ Coarser sand than Siesta Beach

🌊 Crescent Beach — Family Favorite

Stretching between Siesta Beach and Turtle Beach, Crescent Beach is less crowded and perfect for families who want more space. The southern end at Point of Rocks is one of the best snorkeling spots on Florida's Gulf Coast.

  • ✅ Less crowded than Siesta main beach
  • ✅ Point of Rocks — amazing snorkeling
  • ✅ Wide, open shoreline
  • ✅ Great sunset views
  • ⚠️ No lifeguards — swim at your own risk
  • ⚠️ Limited restroom facilities

🌿 Midnight Pass — Wild & Natural

The southernmost tip of Siesta Key. Undeveloped, wild, and completely free of crowds. Walk south from Turtle Beach to find it. This is Siesta Key before tourism — pure nature.

  • ✅ No crowds
  • ✅ Perfect for kayaking and birdwatching
  • ✅ Stunning natural scenery
  • ⚠️ No facilities — bring everything you need
  • ⚠️ No lifeguards
  • ⚠️ Access via walking from Turtle Beach

🅿️ Parking Tips

  • Arrive before 9am in peak season (January–April). The main Siesta Beach lot fills by 9–10am on weekends and most weekdays.
  • Free main lot has ~800 spaces on Beach Road. It's first-come, first-served — no reservations.
  • Overflow parking is available along Beach Road and on side streets in the Village. Expect a 10–15 min walk to the sand.
  • Turtle Beach parking is much easier — smaller lot but far less demand.
  • Skip it entirely — call Siesta Free Ride at 941-600-8380. We'll drop you off right at the beach entrance and pick you up when you're ready.

📋 Beach Rules

❌ Not Allowed

  • ✅ Alcohol allowed — you may bring alcoholic beverages to the beach
  • 🚫 No dogs on Siesta Beach (Turtle Beach only)
  • 🚫 No glass containers of any kind
  • 🚫 No fires without a county permit
  • 🚫 No drones without prior authorization
  • 🚫 No metal detecting in designated areas

✅ Allowed

  • ✅ Tents and umbrellas (must allow 6ft walkway)
  • ✅ Volleyball — courts provided at Siesta Beach
  • ✅ Kayaks and paddleboards (launch from water's edge)
  • ✅ Dogs on leash at Turtle Beach
  • ✅ Snorkeling at Point of Rocks / Crescent Beach
  • ✅ Fishing from the shore (license required)
