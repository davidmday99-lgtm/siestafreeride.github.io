Today at Siesta Key

May 31 brings hot, humid conditions to Siesta Key with a tropical twist—scattered afternoon thunderstorms are possible! Morning temperatures start around 80°F but climb to a warm 87°F by afternoon. The Gulf water remains a comfortable 82°F, perfect for cooling off. Winds increase to 7-10 mph as the day progresses, often preceding afternoon shower activity typical for late May in Florida.

This weather pattern is completely normal for the transition into summer, when sea breezes collide with warm, moist air to create brief but intense afternoon thunderstorms. The key is timing your beach activities to take advantage of the beautiful morning hours while being prepared for potential afternoon rain.

🏖️ Water & Beach Conditions

The 82°F Gulf water provides excellent relief from the warm air temperatures. Morning conditions will be calm with gentle 1-2 foot waves, but waves may increase slightly in the afternoon as winds pick up ahead of potential shower activity. Water clarity remains good in the morning but may become temporarily reduced if afternoon storms stir up sediment.

Pro Tip: Plan your main beach activities for the morning hours (7 AM - 2 PM) when conditions are most stable and comfortable. Pack a lightweight rain jacket or poncho for potential afternoon showers, and have a backup indoor plan ready. Remember that Florida thunderstorms are typically brief (30-60 minutes) but can be intense with lightning—always seek shelter when storms approach!

Sunset tonight is at 8:18 PM. If afternoon storms clear out, you could be rewarded with spectacular sunset colors as the clouds break apart. Always check local weather radar before heading to the beach and monitor conditions throughout the day.

🎯 Things to Do Today

Water Activities

Morning is prime time for swimming, paddleboarding, and all water activities. The calm morning conditions and warm 82°F water create ideal circumstances. Avoid water activities during afternoon thunderstorms due to lightning danger—wait at least 30 minutes after the last thunder is heard before returning to the water.

Beach Relaxation

Set up your beach camp with a sturdy umbrella that can handle increased afternoon winds. Morning beach time is perfect for reading, sunbathing (with proper protection), and beach games. Have a backup plan for indoor activities like visiting Siesta Key Village shops, art galleries, or restaurants if afternoon storms develop.

Local Events & Activities

Many businesses offer flexible scheduling on days with potential rain—call ahead to confirm hours and availability. Indoor dining at waterfront restaurants with large windows allows you to enjoy ocean views while staying dry if needed. Consider booking activities like boat tours for morning hours when conditions are most stable.
