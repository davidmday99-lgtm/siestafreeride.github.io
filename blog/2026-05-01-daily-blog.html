☀️ Siesta Key Beach Report: Friday, May 1, 2026

Published May 1, 2026 • ☀️ Beach Day Forecast

Good morning, Siesta Key! It's Friday, May 1st, and we're looking at a beautiful beach day unfolding across our beloved barrier island. Whether you're planning a morning swim, an afternoon beach picnic, or just soaking up the Gulf vibes, here's everything you need to know to make the most of your day at Siesta Key.

🌡️ Weather & Atmospheric Conditions

Current conditions: The morning started cool at 75°F with partly cloudy skies—perfect for that early beach walk or sunrise coffee. Humidity is moderate at 84%, and we're seeing light 3 mph winds from the south-southwest.

The afternoon outlook: As we head into the afternoon, expect temperatures climbing to a comfortable high of 87°F with increasing sunshine and mostly clear skies. The heat index will feel closer to 95°F at the warmest point, so sunscreen and hydration are non-negotiable. Wind speeds will pick up to 10-12 mph by afternoon, which means smoother conditions for anyone thinking about paddleboarding or kayaking.

Low tonight: Dropping to 71°F, perfect sleeping weather with clear skies overhead.

UV Index: Reaching level 11 at peak—that's extreme. SPF 50+ and reapplication every 2 hours is your move today.

🌊 Water & Beach Conditions

Gulf water temperature: A refreshing 76°F—warm enough for comfortable swimming but still energizing. Perfect for building beach days that don't overheat you.

Wave and surf conditions: Expect calm 1-2 foot waves—ideal for families, beginners, and anyone just wanting to wade in and float. This is the kind of Gulf water that feels like bathwater when you first wade in. No significant swell, no heavy shore break.

Visibility and clarity: Gulf waters are looking clear with good visibility. No red tide reports for Siesta Key today. No elevated jellyfish activity reported. No rip current warnings in effect.

Overall beach vibe: This is a classic May setup—warm, calm, family-friendly. The kind of day where locals and visitors alike spend the whole afternoon without complaints.

Tonight's full moon is nearly at maximum illumination (99% waxing gibbous)—nearly perfect for an evening beach walk. Sunset is at 8:03 PM with moonrise at 8:19 PM. If you're a night-beach person, stay late and experience Siesta Key bathed in moonlight. It's magic.

🎉 What's Happening Around Siesta Key

First Friday of May: The Siesta Village is bustling with energy on Friday afternoons—restaurants opening their patios, shops staying open late, and the whole island shifting into weekend mode. If you're hitting the beach early and want dinner afterward, Main Street and Beach Road are your stages.

Afternoon beach volleyball: Friday afternoons draw pickup volleyball games at the main beach volleyball courts—usually around 3-5 PM. Great for meeting locals and joining a casual game.

Happy hour specials: Most Siesta Key restaurants and beach bars are running their Friday happy hours starting around 4-5 PM. Perfect for transitioning from beach day to evening out.
