Daily Update - April 24, 2026

April 24, 2026•3 min read

Happy Friday, Siesta Key! The weekend is here and the island is looking beautiful. Whether you're a local wrapping up the work week or a visitor who just touched down at SRQ, this is one of those gorgeous late-April evenings that reminds you why Florida's Gulf Coast is so special. Here's your Friday briefing.

 Today's Weather

Friday delivered exactly the kind of day you hope for in late April on Siesta Key. Current conditions as of this evening: 78°F and sunny, feeling like 80°F with a gentle west-southwest breeze at 9 mph. Humidity is sitting at a comfortable 56% — nothing sticky, nothing muggy.

Today's high climbed to 89°F with a low of 64°F overnight. Sunrise was at 6:57 AM and sunset hit at 7:59 PM, giving you over 13 hours of daylight. The UV index hit 9 today, so sunscreen is absolutely essential if you're still out on the water or catching golden-hour photos on the beach.

Weekend forecast looks equally great: Saturday is expected to be mostly sunny with a high around 75°F, and Sunday similar with temps in the mid-70s. Light winds and clear skies all weekend long. Perfect beach weather.

What's Happening in Siesta Key This Weekend

Friday Night in the Village — Siesta Key Village comes alive on Friday evenings. The outdoor seating areas at Gilligan's, The Hub Baja Grill, and Obi's Steak & Sushi fill up fast. Live music pops up at different spots throughout the night. Get there early if you want a good table, or better yet — book a ride with Siesta Free Ride and skip the parking search entirely.

Sunset at Siesta Beach — Nothing beats the world-famous quartz-sand beach at dusk. The white sand stays cool underfoot even after a hot afternoon, and tonight's sunset came right around 7:59 PM. Grab a spot near Beach Access 2 or the Pavilion for the best views. The pavilion area also has restrooms and outdoor showers if you're coming straight from the water.

Sea Turtle Season Is Here — Sea turtle nesting season runs from May through October along Florida's Gulf Coast, and that means right now is the time to be mindful on the beach. If you're out after dark, remember: fill in any holes you dig, knock down sandcastles, and keep beach lights dim or off so hatchlings can find their way to the water. It's a small thing that makes a big difference for local ecosystems.

Weekend Farmers Market — Sunday Morning — The Siesta Key Farmers Market sets up right in the Village area. If you're around Sunday morning, swing by for fresh local produce, artisan baked goods, handmade jewelry, and live acoustic music. It runs from 8 AM to 1 PM and is one of the best ways to spend a relaxed Sunday.
