Beach Conditions

The National Weather Service expects scattered showers and thunderstorms mainly before early afternoon, followed by partly sunny skies. Windy south flow and stronger gusts can make Gulf conditions rougher than they look from shore.

A Rip Current Statement is in effect through 8:00 p.m. Use extra caution, follow lifeguard instructions, and avoid entering rough water. When in doubt, enjoy the shoreline and save the swim for calmer conditions.

Regional NOAA buoy data puts offshore Gulf water near 87°F. The water is warm, but the combination of heat, wind, and storms means hydration, shade, and a flexible plan matter today.

Start With the Beach

A Tuesday on Siesta Key is made for settling into island time. Pack water, sunscreen, and a light cover-up, then let the day unfold between the beach, the Village, and your favorite local stops.

Summer conditions can change quickly, so check the sky before heading out and keep a flexible plan for any passing showers.
