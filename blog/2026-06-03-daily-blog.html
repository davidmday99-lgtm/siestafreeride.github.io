Today at Siesta Key

June 3 brings peak summer heat to Siesta Key! Temperatures will climb to a hot 90°F with high humidity, creating heat index values that feel like 98-102°F. The Gulf water has warmed to a very comfortable 84°F—providing excellent relief from the intense heat. Winds increase to 9-12 mph throughout the day, often preceding scattered afternoon thunderstorms that are typical for early June in Florida.

This represents classic peak summer weather, when hot, humid air masses create ideal conditions for brief but intense afternoon thunderstorms. The key to enjoying today safely is strategic planning—maximize your morning beach time and be prepared to seek shelter if afternoon storms develop.

🏖️ Water & Beach Conditions

At 84°F, the Gulf water feels like a warm bath—perfect for extended swimming sessions to escape the 90°F air temperatures. Morning conditions will be relatively calm with gentle 2-3 foot waves, but wave height may increase slightly in the afternoon as winds pick up ahead of potential storm activity. Water clarity remains good in the morning but may become temporarily reduced if storms stir up sediment.

Pro Tip: Heat safety is absolutely critical today! Plan your main beach activities for early morning hours (6 AM - 1 PM) when conditions are most manageable. Stay well-hydrated with 3+ liters of water per person, use high-SPF waterproof sunscreen every 60-90 minutes, and seek substantial shade frequently. For afternoon storms, have a backup indoor plan and remember to wait 30 minutes after the last thunder before returning to water activities.

Sunset tonight is at 8:19 PM. If afternoon storms clear out, you'll be rewarded with spectacular sunset colors. Always monitor local weather radar and be prepared to adjust your plans quickly if storm conditions develop.

🎯 Things to Do Today

Water Activities

Early morning is prime time for swimming, paddleboarding, and all water activities. The warm 84°F water provides welcome relief from the heat. Avoid all water activities during afternoon thunderstorms due to lightning danger—water is an excellent conductor of electricity, making it extremely hazardous during storms.

Beach Relaxation

Set up your beach camp with maximum shade—a large umbrella, pop-up tent, or natural dune vegetation. Early morning beach time is ideal for reading, light sunbathing (with proper protection), and beach games. Have a solid backup plan for indoor activities like visiting Siesta Key Village shops, art galleries, museums, or air-conditioned restaurants if afternoon storms develop.

Local Events & Activities

Many businesses offer flexible scheduling on hot, storm-prone days—call ahead to confirm hours and availability. Indoor dining at waterfront restaurants with large windows allows you to enjoy ocean views while staying cool and dry if needed. Consider booking morning activities like fishing charters, dolphin tours, or glass-bottom boat tours when conditions are most stable.
