Today at Siesta Key

June 2 brings classic Florida summer weather to Siesta Key! Morning temperatures start around 82°F but climb to a hot 89°F by afternoon. The Gulf water remains a very warm 83°F—perfect for cooling off from the heat. Winds increase to 8-12 mph as the day progresses, often preceding the afternoon thunderstorms that are typical for early June in Florida.

This weather pattern represents peak summer conditions, when sea breezes collide with hot, humid air to create brief but intense afternoon thunderstorms. The key to enjoying today is timing your activities for the beautiful morning hours while being prepared for potential afternoon storms that usually clear by evening.

🏖️ Water & Beach Conditions

The 83°F Gulf water provides excellent relief from the hot air temperatures. Morning conditions will be relatively calm with gentle 2-3 foot waves, but waves may increase slightly in the afternoon as winds pick up ahead of storm activity. Water clarity remains good in the morning but may become temporarily reduced if afternoon storms stir up sediment.

Pro Tip: Plan your main beach activities for the morning hours (7 AM - 2 PM) when conditions are most stable and comfortable. Pack a lightweight rain jacket or poncho for potential afternoon storms, and have a backup indoor plan ready. Remember that Florida thunderstorms are typically brief (30-60 minutes) but can be intense with lightning—always seek shelter when storms approach and wait 30 minutes after the last thunder before returning to water activities!

Sunset tonight is at 8:19 PM. If afternoon storms clear out, you could be rewarded with spectacular sunset colors as the clouds break apart. Always check local weather radar before heading to the beach and monitor conditions throughout the day.

🎯 Things to Do Today

Water Activities

Morning is prime time for swimming, paddleboarding, and all water activities. The warm 83°F water and calm morning conditions create ideal circumstances. Avoid all water activities during afternoon thunderstorms due to lightning danger—water conducts electricity, making it extremely dangerous during storms.

Beach Relaxation

Set up your beach camp with a sturdy umbrella that can handle increased afternoon winds. Morning beach time is perfect for reading, sunbathing (with proper protection), and beach games. Have a backup plan for indoor activities like visiting Siesta Key Village shops, art galleries, or restaurants if afternoon storms develop.

Local Events & Activities

Many businesses offer flexible scheduling on days with potential storms—call ahead to confirm hours and availability. Indoor dining at waterfront restaurants with large windows allows you to enjoy ocean views while staying dry if needed. Consider booking morning activities like boat tours when conditions are most stable.
